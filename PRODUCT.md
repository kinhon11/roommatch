# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Vietnamese tenants (age 18-35, students and young professionals) searching for affordable rooms in major cities. They browse on mobile during commutes and on desktop at home. Landlords managing 1-10 properties, posting listings and responding to inquiries. Admin moderators reviewing content. All users expect a Vietnamese-language interface that feels trustworthy and efficient.

## Product Purpose

RoommieMatch connects room seekers with verified landlords through a moderated marketplace. Listings are admin-reviewed before publishing. The platform supports real-time chat, roommate matching (shared-room requests), appointment scheduling, AI-generated descriptions, favorites, and reviews. Success means a tenant finds a room they trust within one session.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, approachable, efficient. The interface should feel like a reliable friend who knows the local rental market, not a flashy startup or a government portal.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with dark themes and neon accents (not a developer tool)
- Overloaded real estate platforms with banner ads and cluttered sidebars (Batdongsan.com.vn visual noise)
- Flat, unstyled CRUD apps that feel like homework projects
- Heavy glassmorphism, gradient text, or decorative animations that add no information

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through clarity**: every listing, status, and action should be immediately understandable. No ambiguity.
2. **Mobile-first density**: information should be scannable at phone width without scrolling past filler.
3. **Quiet confidence**: the UI recedes so the room photos, prices, and locations stand out. Design serves the content.
4. **Vietnamese context**: currency (VNĐ), city names, and cultural norms (no aggressive upselling) shape every decision.
5. **One-session resolution**: minimize friction between "I need a room" and "I contacted a landlord."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Touch targets 44px on mobile. No color-only status indicators (pair with text or icons). Support reduced motion via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
